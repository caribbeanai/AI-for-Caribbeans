# Climate Change

The Caribbean is on the front line of climate change. Stronger hurricanes, rising seas, bleached reefs, salinised wells. AI can help us prepare, respond, and recover.

## Projects

- [hurricane_intensity.py](hurricane_intensity.py) Predict peak intensity from early storm data.
- [sea_level_trend.py](sea_level_trend.py) Linear and seasonal trends at island tide stations.
- [reef_bleaching_risk.py](reef_bleaching_risk.py) Rolling sea surface temperature anomaly to risk score.

## Why this matters

- Hurricane damages can erase a single year of GDP growth for small islands.
- Sea level rise of 30 cm threatens roads, ports, and aquifers across our region.
- Coral reefs protect coastlines and feed fisheries. Their loss is a national security issue.

## Data sources

- NOAA HURDAT2 for hurricane history.
- PSMSL (Permanent Service for Mean Sea Level) for tide records.
- NOAA Coral Reef Watch for sea surface temperature.
- Local meteorological offices in Jamaica, Trinidad and Tobago, Barbados, the Bahamas.

## Caribbean specific lens

- Damage models trained on Florida or Texas data tend to overestimate insurance value at risk in our region and underestimate population exposure. Build local baselines.
- Communication matters as much as prediction. Build outputs in the languages and channels people actually use, including WhatsApp and community radio scripts.
