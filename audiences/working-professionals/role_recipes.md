# Role recipes

Copy, paste, edit. Each recipe has a prompt and a tip.

## Accountant, variance commentary

```
You are a senior accountant in Kingston. I will paste a profit and loss statement.
Write a clear variance commentary for management. Compare actuals to budget.
Flag items above 10 percent variance. Use plain English. Use JMD where amounts appear.
```

Tip: never paste real customer data. Strip names and account numbers first.

## Lawyer, contract summary

```
You are a Trinidad and Tobago attorney. Summarise the following contract.
Output: parties, term, payment terms, termination, governing law, key risks for the buyer.
Flag anything unusual for a regional commercial agreement.
```

Tip: AI is a fast junior associate, not a senior partner. Always review.

## Banker, customer email

```
You are a relationship manager at a Caribbean commercial bank. Draft a polite reply
to this customer email. Tone: professional, warm, no jargon. Sign off with my name.
Customer email is below.
```

Tip: paste your own signature template. Models drift in tone.

## Teacher, CSEC lesson plan

```
You are a CSEC English teacher in Barbados. Build a 60 minute lesson plan on
figurative language using Kamau Brathwaite's poem "Calypso". Include warmup,
direct instruction, group activity, independent practice, exit ticket. CXC aligned.
```

Tip: ask for a marking rubric in the same prompt.

## Nurse, patient leaflet

```
You are a public health nurse in Saint Lucia. Write a one page patient leaflet
on managing Type 2 diabetes with a Caribbean diet. Use plain English at grade 6
reading level. Include foods to enjoy, foods to limit, and a sample day of meals
using local food: green fig, breadfruit, callaloo, saltfish.
```

Tip: always check medical content with a clinician.

## Hotel manager, review reply

```
You are the GM of a boutique hotel in Negril. Reply to this guest review.
If positive, thank warmly and mention one detail from the review. If negative,
acknowledge, apologise, state action taken, and invite them back. Sign Sarah Brown.
```

Tip: keep a reply log. Patterns reveal real operational issues.

## Farmer, pest identification

```
I am a farmer in Saint Vincent. Here is a description of damage on my dasheen leaves:
small holes, yellowing edges, slow growth over two weeks. What pests or diseases are
most likely? List three. For each, give a low cost, organic first treatment.
```

Tip: combine this with a photo upload if your tool supports vision.

## Civil servant, policy brief

```
You are a senior policy analyst in the Ministry of Tourism, Antigua and Barbuda.
Draft a two page brief on the case for an AI based demand forecasting system for
cruise arrivals. Include background, options, costs, risks, recommendation.
```

Tip: end every brief with three numbered recommendations. Decision makers love three.
