# AI for Kids (ages 7 to 12)

Welcome, young captain. AI is just a clever helper that learns from examples. You teach it, and it gets better. This guide turns AI into games and stories you know.

## What you will learn

1. What AI is, told as a story about a parrot named Patrick from Petit Martinique.
2. How computers learn from examples, using mangoes and ackees.
3. Your first AI project, a sorter that tells the difference between a Julie mango and an East Indian mango.
4. How to talk to an AI like a friend, with manners.

## Lesson 1, Patrick the parrot learns

Patrick lives in a yard with a tamarind tree. Every morning, Granny gives him a treat when he says "good morning". After many mornings, Patrick says it before Granny opens the door. Patrick did not study a book. He learned from examples. AI learns the same way. Show it many photos of a coconut, and it learns to spot a coconut.

## Lesson 2, the mango game (no code)

Print or draw twenty mangoes. Ten are Julie, round and small. Ten are East Indian, long and yellow. Mix them up. Show them one by one to a friend who has never seen mangoes. After ten, ask them to guess the rest. That is supervised learning. The friend is the model. The first ten mangoes are the training data. The rest are the test.

## Lesson 3, your first Python code

Open `kids_mango_sorter.py` in this folder. Run it. Change a number. Run again. See what happens. That is how every programmer learns.

```bash
python audiences/kids/kids_mango_sorter.py
```

## Lesson 4, talking to AI like a friend

When you talk to ChatGPT or Claude, be clear and polite. Ask one thing at a time. If the answer is wrong, say so and ask again.

Try these prompts:
- "Tell me a bedtime story set in Speightstown, Barbados, about a turtle and a flying fish."
- "Explain how a hurricane forms, like I am ten years old."
- "Quiz me on Caribbean capitals."

## Safety rules

1. Never share your real name, address, or school with an AI.
2. If something feels wrong, tell a grown up.
3. AI can be wrong. Always check with a parent, a teacher, or a book.
