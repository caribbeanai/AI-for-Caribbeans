# AI for Retirees

You have time, wisdom, and stories. AI gives you a patient helper that never tires. This guide is gentle, with large steps and no jargon.

## What AI can do for you

1. Write letters, emails, and birthday messages.
2. Help you read and understand health information.
3. Read aloud to you.
4. Translate between English and Spanish, French, or Dutch for our neighbours in Cuba, Haiti, the DR, Martinique, Curaçao.
5. Help you stay safe online.
6. Keep your mind sharp with puzzles and conversation.

## Getting started in three steps

### Step 1, open a free AI assistant

On your phone or computer, go to chat.openai.com or claude.ai. Sign up with your email.

### Step 2, try your first task

Type this:

```
I am a retiree in [your country]. Please write a short, warm birthday note
for my grandchild, age 8. Mention that I love them and miss them.
```

Press enter. Read what comes back. If you want changes, just say so:

```
Make it shorter. Add a joke about a parrot.
```

### Step 3, build a small habit

Use it once a day for one week. Letters, recipes, questions, anything.

## Useful prompts

- "Explain my new medicine, [name], in simple words. What is it for, when to take it, what to avoid."
- "Write a letter to my landlord about the leaking roof."
- "Quiz me on Caribbean capitals so I can stay sharp."
- "Tell me a story about Anansi the spider for my grandson."
- "Translate this letter from my friend in Aruba into English."

## Staying safe

1. AI is not a doctor or a lawyer. Use it to understand, not to decide.
2. Never share your bank details, ID number, or NIS number with any AI.
3. If you get an email saying you won a prize or your account is in danger, paste it to AI first and ask: "Is this a scam?" AI is good at spotting them.
4. If a "grandchild" calls and the voice sounds odd, ask them a family only question. Voice cloning is real.

## Family activity

Sit with your grandchildren. Ask AI to tell stories about your island when you were young, then correct it where it is wrong. You become the teacher.
