# Lesson 3, Image generation prompt patterns

A good image prompt has six parts: subject, action, setting, mood, style, technical.

## Caribbean specific subjects that work

- Steel pan player at dusk, Queen's Park Savannah, Port of Spain.
- Pitons rising from a calm Caribbean Sea, golden hour.
- Junkanoo dancer mid leap, Bay Street Nassau, fireworks above.
- Market vendor with breadfruit and ackee, Coronation Market, Kingston.
- Catamaran on glass calm water, Tobago Cays, aerial view.

## Mood words that translate well

Sunlit, balmy, vibrant, tropical, sun bleached, lush, salt sprayed.

## Style modifiers

- Photographic: shot on 35mm, natural light, shallow depth of field.
- Editorial: cover of a magazine, double page spread.
- Illustration: gouache, watercolour, bold lines, ink wash.
- Painting: oil on canvas, palette knife textures.

## Avoid

- Generic "Caribbean beach" prompts. They produce stock imagery from anywhere.
- Mentioning living artists by name. Describe the qualities instead.
- Photo realistic faces of real people you do not have permission to depict.

## A template

```
Subject: [WHO/WHAT, age, attire, expression]
Action: [WHAT THEY DO]
Setting: [WHERE, WHEN, WEATHER]
Mood: [TWO WORDS]
Style: [PHOTO OR ILLUSTRATION OR PAINTING]
Technical: [LENS, LIGHT, ASPECT RATIO]
```
