# Lesson 2, History and the Caribbean angle

A short timeline, then how it touches us.

- 1950, Alan Turing asks if a machine can think. He proposes the imitation game.
- 1956, the term Artificial Intelligence is coined at Dartmouth.
- 1980s, expert systems boom and bust.
- 1997, IBM Deep Blue beats Garry Kasparov at chess.
- 2012, deep learning takes off after AlexNet wins ImageNet.
- 2017, the Transformer paper is published. This is the seed of modern LLMs.
- 2022, ChatGPT launches and AI becomes a household word.
- 2024 and onward, agents, multimodal models, on device AI.

## Caribbean angle

The Caribbean has always been a region of adaptation. We took the steel pan from oil drums. We built reggae from sound system culture. We turned tourism into a major industry from a foundation of natural beauty and welcome. AI is the next material. We do not need to invent the foundations to use them well.

Three observations that matter for our region:

1. Most foundation models are trained on data that under represents us. Caribbean English, French, Spanish, Dutch, Papiamento, Sranan, and Kreyol are present but thin. This affects accuracy when we use them as is.
2. The cost of building applications has dropped to the floor. A solo developer in Bridgetown can ship products that compete globally.
3. Our small populations make AI personalisation easier. A few thousand customers is a large enough dataset to learn useful patterns for a local business.
