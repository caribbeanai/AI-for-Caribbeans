# Course 01, Foundations of AI

By the end of this course you will be able to explain what AI is, what kinds exist, where it fits, and where it fails. You will also write your first Python that uses an AI library.

## Lessons

1. [What is AI](lesson_01_what_is_ai.md)
2. [History and the Caribbean angle](lesson_02_history.md)
3. [Types of AI](lesson_03_types.md)
4. [How models learn](lesson_04_how_models_learn.md)
5. [Data, the fuel](lesson_05_data.md)
6. [Ethics and bias in our context](lesson_06_ethics.md)
7. [Your first AI script](first_script.py)

## Scenarios

Read [scenarios.md](scenarios.md) for six small Caribbean stories that test your judgement on when AI fits and when it does not.

## Datasets

See [../../datasets/CATALOG.md](../../datasets/CATALOG.md) for every dataset used in this repo. None are required for foundations, but later courses pull from there constantly.

## Capstone

Make an AI literacy poster for your school, church, or workplace. The poster must answer: What AI can do, what it cannot, and three rules for using it well. Print it. Hang it up.

## Time budget

About six hours total. One lesson per evening.
