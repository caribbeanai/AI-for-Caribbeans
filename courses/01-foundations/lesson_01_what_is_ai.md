# Lesson 1, What is AI

Artificial Intelligence is software that performs tasks we usually associate with human thinking. Reading, writing, seeing, listening, planning, deciding.

We have had AI ideas since the 1950s, but the last decade is when AI got loud. Two things changed: cheap computing power, and giant piles of digital data.

## Three useful categories

1. **Rule based AI.** If this, then that. The phone's autocomplete from 2005 was rule based. A traffic light timer in Port of Spain is, in a small way, rule based.
2. **Machine learning.** The system learns patterns from examples. Most useful AI today is machine learning. Spam filters, fraud detection, demand forecasts.
3. **Generative AI.** The system creates new content: text, images, music, code. ChatGPT and Claude are generative AI.

## Why now matters for us

A teacher in Kingstown, Saint Vincent can prepare lesson plans in minutes. A mango farmer in Saint Mary, Jamaica can identify a pest from a photo. A small hotel in Soufrière can write a guest reply in any language. The Caribbean does not have to wait for these tools. They are here and most of them are free at the entry level.

## What AI is not

- It is not magic.
- It is not always right. It makes confident mistakes.
- It does not understand the world the way you do. It predicts patterns from data.
- It cannot replace your judgement, your relationships, or your culture.
