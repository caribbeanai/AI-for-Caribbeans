# Foundations: Scenarios

Six small scenarios. Each is a story, a question, and a one paragraph guide on how AI fits or does not fit. No coding required.

## Scenario 1: Granny's medicine cabinet, Saint Andrew, Jamaica

Marjorie is 72. Her doctor adds a new tablet to a long list. She does not understand the dosing.

**Question:** Is AI useful here, and what are the limits?

**Guide:** A general LLM can explain what a medicine is and the typical schedule in plain English. It must not replace the pharmacist. Marjorie should still call her local pharmacy to confirm interactions with her existing prescriptions. AI is a literacy tool, not a clinician.

## Scenario 2: The Maracas bake and shark stand, Trinidad

Jermaine has run a stand for ten years. He thinks of building a "WhatsApp ordering bot" because tourists ask for menus.

**Question:** Should he build an AI bot, or a simpler tool?

**Guide:** Start with a one page WhatsApp Business catalogue. If volume grows past 50 orders a day or he hires a second person, an AI bot starts to pay off. Build for the bottleneck, not the buzzword.

## Scenario 3: A bank in Bridgetown asks for a credit model

A small bank wants to score loan applicants automatically. The credit team has 6,000 historical decisions.

**Question:** What goes wrong if they ship a model trained only on those past decisions?

**Guide:** They will encode past biases. Applicants who were rejected never had a chance to prove they would repay, so the model only sees the world of those who got loans. Defences: keep humans in the loop, audit by parish and income bracket, give clear reasons for denial, and keep a rejection appeal path.

## Scenario 4: A school in Castries wants an AI tutor for CSEC

The principal asks: can an LLM serve as a tutor for our students?

**Question:** What does the implementation need so this actually helps grades?

**Guide:** Ground the tutor in the real CXC syllabus and past papers (RAG). Test on real student questions. Track who improved on graded mocks before and after. Without measurement, you will not know if it works.

## Scenario 5: A hurricane is forecast for the Cayman Islands

NEMA's preparedness team is wondering whether to build a damage prediction model from drone imagery.

**Question:** What does it take to actually save lives or property?

**Guide:** A model is the easy part. The hard parts are: who flies the drones, who reviews the AI's flagged areas, how priority cases reach response teams, and how citizens hear about updates. AI without operations is a slide deck.

## Scenario 6: A young creator in Kingstown wants to clone her own voice

She makes voice notes for her followers and wants to scale.

**Question:** Is voice cloning OK here, and what are the rules?

**Guide:** Cloning your own voice is fine. Cloning anyone else's voice requires their explicit, informed consent. Disclose when content uses synthetic voice. Watch out for impersonation scams that may now target your family with your cloned voice.

## Use these in a class

- Read aloud one scenario.
- Discuss in pairs for five minutes.
- Reconvene, agree on the right move.
- Compare with the guide.

None required. These scenarios are for thinking, not coding.
